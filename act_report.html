Udacity Project Act Report

I explored the cleaned twitter-archive-master csv data to answer some questions and create visualization. Some of the columns I cleaned during the wrangling stage are the ones I used for analysis these are timestamp column from which I extracted weekday, month and year, source column from which I extracted the device used to post tweet, doggo stage columns that I combined into one column and on breed prediction columns I renamed columns to something more descriptive and fixed the inconsistencies in the breed names.

Using pandas built in functions I explored the data to answer some questions and get insights. The questions I answered are:

  1. What is the most frequently used device used for posting tweet?

  2. What month are most tweet posted?

  3. How many image predictions are not dogs if all three breed predictions are False and how many are dogs if all three breed predictions are true?

  4. What is the most popular dog stage?

Insights from data

Exploratory analysis of the data showed that tweets are posted from three sources namely; Twitter for iPhone,Twitter Web Client and TweetDeck. Out of 1971 tweets posted, 1932 were posted with an iPhone.

Analysis also showed that most tweets were posted in December which is the 12th month. Out of 1971 tweets posted 414 tweets were posted in the 12th month while 347 tweets were posted in the 11th month.

Exploratory analysis of image prediction based on images tweeted showed that not all images uploaded are dogs, each tweet image has three prediction to dtermine if it is a dog or not. About 540 images have their three dog predictions to be False, this means that 540 images tweeted were predicted not to be dogs.

Analysis of dog stage column shows that most of the dog names are missing, and there are dogs that are classified into multiple dog stages Apart from dogs with missing names and some being classified into multiple stages, doggo dog stage is popular.
